Nations that participated in the invasion of Poland 15 years after the event

The countries that participated in the Poland invasion were 3: Nazi Germany, Slovak Republic, and the Soviet Union.

Nazi Germany

German economic output recovered rapidly in 1946 as plants and mines reopened.

Slovak Republic

After signing the Peace Treaty of Paris, Slovakia lost its so-called independence and was reunified with the Czech Republic.

Soviet Union

At the end of World War II, the Soviet Union occupied Bulgaria, Romania, Hungary, Poland and eastern Germany. Great Britain, the United States, France, and the Soviet Union divided Germany and Berlin into four occupation zones to be administered by the four countries.
